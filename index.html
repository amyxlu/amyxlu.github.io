Amy X. Lu

Vector Institute for Artificial Intelligence | Department of Computer Science, University of Toronto

email: amyxlu [at] cs [dot] toronto [dot] edu

I am broadly interested in developing machine learning methods for the unique needs of biological data. I am especially interested in methods enabling generalization to natural distribution shifts and practical use cases in therapeutics and discovery.

I am currently exploring these interests at insitro, using machine learning to accelerate drug discovery. I completed my Masters at the University of Toronto and the Vector Institute, advised by Alan Moses and Marzyeh Ghassemi. I partially completed my Masters as a Visiting Student Researcher in Anshul Kundaje’s group at Stanford University, as a recipient of the NSERC CGS-MSFSS scholarship.

My research interests were shaped by wonderful mentors at Harvard Medical School, University of Waterloo and École polytechnique fédérale de Lausanne. I work with Research to the People, a non-profit connecting patients of complex diseases, researchers, and industry partners. In my spare time, I enjoy making piano covers, visiting museums, and eating 90% dark chocolate.
