Amy X. Lu

I'm a first-year PhD student at UC Berkeley and BAIR, working with Nilah Ioannidis, Yun Song, and Sergey Levine.

Previously, I was at insitro, applying machine learning to accelerate drug discovery. I completed my MSc at the University of Toronto, advised by Alan Moses and Marzyeh Ghassemi. During my MSc, I was a visitor in Anshul Kundaje's group at Stanford University, as a recipient of the NSERC CGS-MSFSS award.

Broadly, I'm interested in developing artificial intelligence methods for the unique needs of biological data. I'm especially interested in enabling generalization to natural distribution shifts, and use cases in therapeutic biomolecular design. I completed my undergrad at the University of Waterloo, and was a researcher at Harvard Medical School prior to starting my Masters.
